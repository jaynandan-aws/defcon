Salesforce to JIRA integration- Part 1

By Jaynandan Prasad 1 Comments

There has always been a generic business requirement to pull/push the information from JIRA to Salesforce and vice-versa. There are few connectors available in apexchange.com and Atlassian market place.  However, we found them to be costly and over-featured as well, hence we decided to build our own native Salesforce to JIRA integration using REST API.

Here’s a quick snap of the business requirement which were completed in different phases.

Phase 1: Pull information from JIRA to Salesforce on a Visulaforce page layout in the Case object using OAuth2.0 authentication.

Business requirement: As a Technical Support Representative I want to update the customers about the status of the bug in the product.  It would be great if I can have all the bug information handy in Case record so that I do not have to toggle between Salesforce and JIRA.

Expected Result:

  1. Decrease the response time on any Case
  2. Reduce cost by not issuing a different JIRA licenses to the Technical Support Reps.

 

Phase 2: Salesforce- JIRA integration to Push the information from Salesforce to JIRA and create a bug out of the Case record in JIRA system.  And then populate the URL of the newly created bug in the Case record in SF.

Business requirement: As a Scrum Master/ Product Owner/Reporter I should be able to create bug in JIRA from Salesforce by pushing the information in the Case record and getting back the URL of the bug in the case record.

Expected Result: creation of the bug in JIRA using the case record. Avoid creating duplicate bugs

*There were other minute project details which cannot be shared in the public forum
